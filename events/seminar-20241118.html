Seminar: Spatial analysis for understanding and improving fire service response in West Midlands, UK
Monday 18th November 2024, 11:00 - 12:00

GERI building 1.02
Liverpool John Moores University
3 Byrom St, Liverpool L3 3AF

Dr Huanfa Chen Associate Professor in Spatial Data Science (UCL CASA)
Deputy Departmental Tutor
Associate Fellow of Royal Institute of Navigation (AFRIN)
Associate Editor, Annals of GIS

Abstract: Fire and rescue services in the UK have faced challenges of downgraded services and delayed responses to fire incidents. Understanding the spatial pattern and factors of fire service quality is essential for enhancing these services. In this presentation, I will discuss metrics and methods for understanding the barriers of place-based fire service quality, using geospatial machine learning techniques. Then, I will present the preliminary results from 15-year records of fire incidents and response from West Midlands Fire Services.

Speaker: Dr Huanfa Chen is an Associate Professor in Spatial Data Science at the UCL Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis (CASA). He holds a PhD degree in GeoInformatics from UCL SpaceTimeLab, and BSc and MSc from Peking University. His research interests span spatial optimisation and GeoAI, with applications in public health, fire services, and transport research. He currently serves as associate editor of Annals of GIS and reviewers for multiple academic journals. He has published over 15 articles on international journals relating to GIS, urban analytics, and public health.

Presented by Maritime Research & Innovation Lab (MarRI-Lab)
